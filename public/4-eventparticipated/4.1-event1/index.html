Event 1

Event Review: May 9th Tech Event

Purpose of the Event

  • Share “brain-hacking” methods to build effective study and work habits, avoiding social media distractions.
  • Guide architectural design and cost optimization on Cloud/AI.
  • Prepare the mindset and skill sets needed for students/freshers in the AI era (AI Ready Freshers).
  • Introduce modern software development methods (Bmad) and Context Engineering techniques when working with AI.

Speakers

  • Mr. Hoang Long - Shared topic: Addicted to learning like addicted to social media.
  • Mr. Thinh - Shared topic: Automating prompts for AI.
  • Mr. Khang - Shared topic: Why haven’t you worked yet - AI ready freshers.
  • Ms. Thao - Shared topic: AI technologies and the Bmad Framework.

Key Highlights

Mr. Hoang Long: Addicted to learning like addicted to social media

  • Decoding the Brain: The brain prioritizes social media over learning because social media provides instant rewards (dopamine), triggers curiosity, and consumes very little energy. Conversely, learning requires long focus, delayed rewards, and does not give instant gratification.
  • Turning Learning into a “Dopamine Game”: The key to motivation is recognizing the value of the reward for each learning task. We need to create a clear reward/penalty system (e.g., creating a lucky draw box after each study session) to build a reward loop.
  • 3 “Brain-Hacking” Tips:
    • Use Fear of Loss to Learn: Humans tend to fear losing progress (fear of missing out/losing) more than enjoying gains. Create a study “streak” to establish a fear of breaking the streak.
    • Chunk Study Time: Trick the brain’s core by dividing study time into 10-minute blocks, repeated multiple times, rather than studying continuously for hours.
    • Create Feedback from Others: Reward yourself with a 10-minute break after completing a task. Apply social media mechanisms to learning, such as streaks, leaderboards, and creating anticipation for the next milestone.
  • Conclusion: Winners are not those who are always motivated, but those who know how to turn motivation into a habit.

Mr. Thinh: Automating prompts for AI

  • Presented entirely in English with a professional, international style.
  • Detailed the project architecture and how different services connect with each other.
  • Analyzed the causes and architectural designs that make operational costs for the entire project extremely cheap.
  • Deep-dived into structures and methods to build effective automated prompts for AI systems.

Mr. Khang: Why haven’t you worked yet - Mindset for “AI Ready Freshers”

  • Overcoming the Fear of AI: The AI boom makes students feel overwhelmed and afraid of being replaced. The solution is to focus on core values that never change and master 5 foundational services to get started.
  • Principles of Using AI: Use AI but truly understand the nature. There is no absolute right or wrong, only better. Always question the results generated by AI. Do not hesitate to ask the community for help and dare to make mistakes to learn.
  • Integrity: The vital element of teamwork. Show everyone that you “follow through to the end” to build firm trust with teammates in the project.
  • 3 Work Circles: Things you love (Love) - Things that bring benefits (Benefits) - Things you have to do (Have to do).
  • 5 Benefits of Working (Benefits): Salary, Experience, Network, Knowledge/Skills, Personal Growth.
  • Enterprise Evaluation Criteria: Attitude, Competence, Experience, Life Experience, and Core Talent.

Ms. Thao: Software Development Methods & Bmad Framework

  • Warning about “Vibe Coding”: Coding using AI based purely on intuition (Vibe coding) is only suitable for demos, not production-ready software. Overreliance on it causes developers to forget core foundational knowledge.
  • The Shift: Shift mindset from “Prompting” (simple questioning) to “Context Engineering” (engineering context) to provide sufficient data for the AI to understand the whole system.
  • Introducing Bmad (on GitHub): A framework that perfectly fits current software development standards.
  • Standard Workflow with AI:
    • Assign roles to AI, defining which AI member handles what.
    • Create a closed pipeline: Brainstorm chat -> Initialize project -> When enough scenarios/data are gathered, move to Coder -> Move to Tester -> Loop until complete.
    • Vital note: Use separate AI chat sessions for each task/role. Do not mix everything in one chat to avoid confusing the AI’s context.

Lessons Learned

Mindset & Soft Skills

  • Understood the brain’s mechanism regarding procrastination and how to apply “Dopamine” to gamify learning, turning it into a daily habit.
  • Deeply realized the value of “Integrity” in teamwork – committing to follow through to build trust.
  • Shaped the mindset of a Fresher in the AI era: Do not panic before the vast pool of knowledge, focus on foundations (5 key services), and maintain critical thinking to verify AI-generated results.

AI Architecture & Techniques

  • Understood the essence of Context Engineering when interacting with LLMs to build optimal and cost-effective automated prompt flows.
  • Learned to eliminate unstructured “Vibe coding” and apply the Bmad method to build production-ready projects.
  • Mastered role-playing configurations for AI: setting up distinct chats for brainstorming, coding, and testing to form a closed, consistent software development pipeline.

Application to Work & Current Project

  • Personal Time Management: Applied chunking (10 minutes/block) and streaks to maintain progress on AWS Labs and AI Riddle Generator tasks without losing motivation.
  • Interacting with Amazon Bedrock: In the internship project, I will shift from basic prompting to Context Engineering. Instead of general questions, I will explicitly set the Role (e.g., a children’s education specialist) and Context so the AI returns precise JSON for riddle generation.
  • Source Code Management: Avoid using a single chat session to code the entire project. I will split it into separate sessions for Frontend (React), Backend (Lambda), and Database (DynamoDB) following Ms. Thao’s advice to prevent code loops and errors.

Event Experience

  • Participating in the May 9th event was an enlightening session, connecting the dots between personal development and modern technical programming.
    • Unexpected Mindset Shift: Mr. Hoang Long’s sharing helped me realize how I fell into the “instant gratification” trap of social media, and gave me scientific ways to hack my own brain.
    • Practical & Professional: Mr. Thinh’s 100% English presentation of a real architecture inspired me, showing the importance of language skills and cost-optimization in the cloud.
    • Relieving Pressure: Mr. Khang helped freshers like me relieve the fear of AI replacement. The message to “focus on foundations” and “dare to make mistakes” gave me confidence during my internship.
    • Changing Workflow: Ms. Thao’s knowledge on Bmad and Context Engineering immediately changed how I use AI tools. The role-play and pipeline chunking were keys to apply directly to my current AI Riddle Generator project.
